How Girish Karnad's Tughlaq walks among us even today

Muhammad-bin Tughlaq, the eighteenth sultan of Delhi, ruled the region from 1325 to 1351, but a play based on his life’s eccentricities, choices and peculiarities continues to hold sway over theatre lovers simply by being able to find resonance with the audience over six decades later. Tughlaq, Girish Karnad’s iconic play, that has seen hundreds of productions over the past 60 years and multiple translations will complete 60 years in December. When it was published, this 13-scene play, about the turbulent rule of Tughlaq, was interpreted as an allegory on the Nehruvian era. Karnad freely blended fact and fiction to give the story a contemporary relevance. The play then represented the hopes and disappointments in the political life of the Nehruvian era in Indian politics.
